My Manifesto

Who I Am

I am a competitor. Winning isn't just something I want, it's something I'm wired for. I'm loyal to my team, my family, and myself, and no matter what situation I'm put in, I find a way to make the most of it. Through all of it, God is with me. Not as a reason but as a constant. He's there in the grind, in the hard days, and in everything I'm building toward.

I am a son, a partner, and a man who knows exactly where he came from. The people in my life don't just motivate me, they're built into how I carry myself every single day. I am loyal, and I genuinely care about the people around me. Everything I'm building is as much for them as it is for me. I'm not perfect, but I show up every day trying to be better than I was yesterday, as a person, as a teammate, and as someone the people I love can count on. The standard I hold myself to goes way beyond what happens on a field.

Why I'm Doing This

Mom. She did it alone. Multiple jobs, every sacrifice, never once let me feel like I was missing anything. She was always there when I needed her, no matter what. The day she never has to work again because of something I built is the day I know I made it.

Dad. He's 73 and still showing up to work. That stops because of me. He was there for me through the hardest stretches of my life, and I owe him the same energy back. I want him to live long enough to see everything I become and actually benefit from it. That's not a goal. That's a promise.

Grammy. She passed in 2015, but she never left. Every day after school while my parents worked, I came home to her. We did homework together, laughed together, just existed together in that living room. She helped shape who I am. Everything I do carries a piece of her with it.

Ciara. I've known her since sixth grade. We've been through everything together, the highs, the lows, all of it, and she's still who I want standing next to me at the end of it. I want to build a life where we're both happy, a beautiful family, beautiful kids, and a home we're proud of. She deserves that. I'm going to give it to her.

This Season's Targets

Every game I'm working toward double digit goals and pushing my assists there too. The floor is 5 goals and 5 assists. That's not the goal, that's the minimum.

By the end of this season I'm walking away with Big South All-Conference First Team, Offensive Player of the Year, and Conference Newcomer of the Year. New team, new conference, new opponents. None of that changes what I'm here to do.

I'm putting PC on the map. By the time this season is over, Presbyterian College isn't just a name people scroll past. People across the country will know what we're doing here.

And I want people to feel it. Family, friends, my community, the people who have watched me grow up. I want them to see the success and feel proud of what I've built.

The Bigger Picture

This season is the turning point. If I go out and perform the way I know I can, I'm going pro. That's the dream and I'm not letting go of it.

While I'm doing that, I'm finishing my undergrad and then coming back for one more year to get my MBA. I'm building myself on and off the field at the same time.

If soccer doesn't take me where I want to go, I'm still winning. I'm getting into finance and computer science, landing a six figure job, and building a life worth being proud of. A family, two or three kids, and a foundation I built myself.

Either way, I'm not losing.

My Non-Negotiables

I train my body every single day to get better physically. No shortcuts, no excuses.

I take care of my body. Treatment and recovery are not optional. I learned the hard way what happens when your body breaks down and I will not let that happen again.

I work on my mind and my emotions every day too. Being physically ready means nothing if my headspace is off. I show up mentally and emotionally prepared to operate at my best no matter what the day looks like.

On game day my mind is locked in on one thing. Whoever is lined up across from me is going home knowing they faced somebody. I am better than them and I play like it every single time.

My faith in God never wavers. He brought me through the darkest stretch of my life and I trust Him with everything I'm still working toward.

Who I'm Proving Wrong

My coach looked me in the eye and told me I was going to be at the bottom of the roster. He hadn't even seen the new guys play yet and had no idea what the returning players were going to bring either. He had already made up his mind before anyone touched the field. That took something from me before the season even started.

Things got so dark that I got depressed. Soccer is the main reason I chose this school, it's what drives where I go and what I do, and when that got taken from me it took a lot else with it. I carried that weight for a long time and I still showed up every single day.

The people who were loudest when I was winning went quiet when I needed them most. Nobody checked in the way they used to.

I don't have a speech for any of them. They were wrong about who I am and wrong about what I'm capable of and wrong to treat me like I wasn't worth believing in.

This season is my answer. Not for them. For me.

Who I Am and What I Believe

  • I am the best player on any field I step on.
  • I am built for this. My body is strong, my mind is stronger.
  • I survived my darkest years and came out with more hunger than I started with.
  • I am a D1 athlete, a future pro, a son, a teammate, and a man of God.
  • Everything I went through was preparation, not punishment.
  • I belong here.
God didn't bring me through all of that to watch me settle.
